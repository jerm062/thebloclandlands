The Blocland Lands Rulebook

Introduction

This rulebook outlines the basics of playing The Blocland Lands.

Creating a Character

The guide will walk you through choices defining your adventurer.

  1. Religious Belief – choose None, Monotheistic, or Polytheistic.
    • None: speaks only Common.
    • Monotheistic: knows Common and Scripture; chooses Benevolent or Vengeful alignment.
    • Polytheistic: knows Common and Old Tongue; chooses Benevolent or Vengeful alignment.
  2. Family Background – select one option to determine your subclass traits.
  3. Traits – every trait begins at a rating of 6. Subclass traits start at 5, making them easier to use. A trait succeeds on a d6 roll equal to or above its rating.
  4. Starting Gear – you begin with rations, bedroll, tent, pouch, backpack, lantern, and flint box. Additional items may be purchased if the guide allows.
  • Spiritual Leader: Lore, Negotiation, Observation
  • Healer: Healing, Herbcraft, Animal Care
  • Hunter: Hunting, Foraging, Fishing
  • Raider: Intimidation, Tactics, Scavenging
  • Crafter: Repair, Crafting, Appraisal
  • Navigator: Navigation, Cartography, Weather Sense

Record your languages, alignment, traits, and inventory on your character sheet before play begins.

Gameplay

Players describe what their characters attempt while the guide narrates the world and outcomes.

When action outcomes are uncertain, roll a d6 against a trait. A roll equal to or above the trait rating succeeds; the guide narrates the result.

Exploration happens in Travel Points—morning, afternoon, and night—spent on movement, investigation, or rest.

Combat

Combat is turn-based. Roll dice and compare results to determine success.

Equipment & Encumbrance

Starting characters receive:

  • Rations x3
  • Bedroll (Average)
  • Tent (Average)
  • Pouch (+2 slots)
  • Backpack (+4 slots)
  • Lantern (Average)
  • Flint Box

Each character has 12 inventory slots. Backpack adds 4 slots; pouch adds 2. Two extra slots may be overfilled—doing so makes you encumbered, imposing disadvantage on physical tasks and adding +1 TP per hex traveled.

Items with durability start at Average; harsh use can lower them to Poor. Characters with the Repair trait can roll d6—5-6 improves item one step.

Hex Travel

A clear hex requires three Travel Points (morning, afternoon, night), about eight hours total.

During each point, players may:

  • Travel – move forward and spend the point.
  • Explore – guide rolls a d6 to generate features like villages, ruins, or landmarks.
  • Hunt, Forage, or Fish – roll d6; only 6 gathers food or ingredients.
  • Camp – rest and eat; cooking may attract beasts (guide rolls d6).

Camping should occur at night. Skipping camp causes fatigue next day. Rations eaten nightly; missing meals causes hunger.

After each action, the guide secretly rolls the event die:

  1. Wild Encounter
  2. Human Encounter
  3. Environment
  4. Loss
  5. Exhaustion
  6. Discovery

Sin and Nightmares

Characters track Sin. The guide adds sin for immoral acts. At day's end, roll d20 for rest; roll additional \"nightmare\" dice equal to sin points. Rolling both a 1 on rest die and any 1 on nightmare dice denies rest.

Characters without religion roll two rest dice; another player may perform a ritual to remove sin.

Monotheists remove sin by self-flagellating (lose 1d4 HP); Polytheists must leave a fitting tribute, permanently losing the item.
