The Bloc Land Lands Rulebook

Introduction

This rulebook outlines the basics of playing The Bloc Land Lands.

Creating a Character

The guide will walk you through several choices that define your adventurer.

  1. Religious Belief – choose None, Monotheistic or Polytheistic.
    • None speaks only Common.
    • Monotheistic knows Common and Scripture and selects a Benevolent or Vengeful alignment.
    • Polytheistic knows Common and Old Tongue and also chooses Benevolent or Vengeful.
  2. Family Background – select one of the options below to determine your subclass traits.
  3. Traits – every trait begins at a rating of 6. The subclass traits chosen above start at 5, making them easier to use. A trait succeeds on a d6 roll equal to or above its rating.
  4. Starting Gear – you begin with rations, bedroll, tent, pouch, backpack, lantern and flint box. Additional items may be purchased from the item shop if the guide allows.
  • Spiritual Leader: Lore, Negotiation, Observation
  • Healer: Healing, Herbcraft, Animal Care
  • Hunter: Hunting, Foraging, Fishing
  • Raider: Intimidation, Tactics, Scavenging
  • Crafter: Repair, Crafting, Appraisal
  • Navigator: Navigation, Cartography, Weather Sense

Record your languages, alignment, traits and inventory on your character sheet before play begins.

Gameplay

Players state what their characters attempt while the guide describes the world and its reactions.

When the outcome of an action is uncertain, roll a d6 against an appropriate trait. A roll equal to or above the trait rating succeeds. The guide interprets the result and narrates the consequences.

Exploration happens in Travel Points—morning, afternoon and night—which can be spent to move, investigate or rest as outlined in the Hex Travel rules.

Combat

Combat is turn based. Roll a die and compare results to determine success.

Equipment & Encumbrance

Starting characters receive:

  • Rations x3
  • Bedroll (Average)
  • Tent (Average)
  • Pouch (+2 slots)
  • Backpack (+4 slots)
  • Lantern (Average)
  • Flint Box

Everyone has 12 inventory slots. A backpack adds 4 and a pouch adds 2. Two additional encumbrance slots may be used beyond this total—no more can be carried. Items in these slots make the character encumbered, causing disadvantage on physical tasks.

While encumbered, traveling across a hex requires one extra Travel Point.

Items with durability begin at Average. Harsh use can drop them to Poor. Characters with the Repair trait may roll a d6—on 5-6 the item improves one step.

Hex Travel

A clear hex requires three Travel Points—morning, afternoon and night—roughly eight hours total.

For each point, players choose one of these tasks:

  • Travel – move forward and spend the point.
  • Explore – the guide rolls a d6 to inspire features such as villages, ruins or landmarks.
  • Hunt, Forage or Fish – each action slot rolls a d6; only a 6 gathers food or ingredients.
  • Camp – rest and eat. Cooking may draw beasts on a d6 roll.

Camping should take place at night. Skipping camp gives a fatigue penalty the next day. Rations are eaten at night as well; failing to do so causes hunger.

After every action the guide secretly rolls the event die:

  1. Wild Encounter
  2. Human Encounter
  3. Environment
  4. Loss
  5. Exhaustion
  6. Discovery

Level Advancement

Characters earn experience points (XP) for exploring new hexes, defeating foes and recovering treasure. Guides may also award XP for important milestones.

When enough XP is gained to reach a new level, choose one trait to advance. The first advancement lets that trait succeed on a roll of 4–6, the second on 3–6 and the third on 2–6. No trait may advance more than three times.

XP required for each level:

  • Level 2 – 100 XP
  • Level 3 – 300 XP
  • Level 4 – 600 XP
  • Level 5 – 1000 XP
  • Level 6 – 1500 XP
  • Level 7 – 2100 XP
  • Level 8 – 2800 XP
  • Level 9 – 3600 XP
  • Level 10 – 4500 XP
  • Level 11 – 5500 XP
  • Level 12 – 6600 XP
  • Level 13 – 7800 XP
  • Level 14 – 9100 XP

Sin and Nightmares

Each character tracks a Sin score on their sheet. The guide may add points when actions defy morality or the character's alignment.

At the end of each day the player rolls a d20 for rest. For every sin point they also roll a "nightmare" die. Rolling both a 1 on the rest die and at least one 1 on the nightmare dice means no rest is gained.

Characters without a religion roll two dice for the rest check and may ask another player for forgiveness—if that player performs a ritual the sin is removed.

Monotheists remove a sin by self-flagellating with a whip, losing 1d4 HP. Polytheists must leave a fitting tribute at a sacred site, permanently losing the offered item.
